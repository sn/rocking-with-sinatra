### Introduction

Before we get going, I must warn that this book is not intended to be a beginners book, it does not take you through the journey of introducing you to the Sinatra DSL from scratch. We dive straight into more advanced topics regarding real world usage, there are no proverbial 'hello world' examples in these pages. I do however use the first 2 chapters to discuss topics you need to understand before tackling the examples further in the book (purely from a practical perspective).

My intention with this book is to keep it concise and throw you straight into the process building useful production software with the Sinatra framework; Software that can go forth and earn.

I highly recommend that you first read through the [Sinatra documentation](https://sinatrarb.com) before starting this journey since its fundamental to understanding Sinatra. Luckily, Sinatra's documentation is short, sweet and easy to understand.

It’s also advisable that you have a fair understanding of the Ruby programming language or most of what’s in this book won’t  make much sense to you.

There are excellent Ruby resources out there, some of my personal favorites are:

1. [Eloquent Ruby, Second Edition](https://pragprog.com/titles/eruby2/eloquent-ruby-second-edition/) by Russ Olsen
2. [Practical Object-Oriented Design in Ruby](https://www.poodr.com/) by Sandi Metz
3. [The Odin Project](https://www.theodinproject.com/) (free, comprehensive full-stack curriculum)

As developers, we all search for that small, comfortable, fast & efficient tool to add to our belt for doing our magic in a quick & predictable way - that's Sinatra in a nutshell and the reason why I've written this book. It's intention is to serve as an educational reference or recipe book showing real world problems and real world solutions in the most elegant way possible.

Sinatra is so simple & straightforward that most online examples never seem to go further than the introductory cruft. At [Underwulf], we use Sinatra for real-time [production systems](https://indiemaker.com) and so do many others.

There seems to be a misconception about Sinatra being better suited for small applications or services, but that's not entirely true.

This book is the result of all the research, lessons & advice we've picked up in the process of building real software for real people using this awesome little tool.

I hope that I can teach you a few new techniques along the way.

All example code can be downloaded from: [https://github.com/sn/rocking-sinatra]

Enjoy!
